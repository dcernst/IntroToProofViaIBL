We analogously define \(\tcboxmath{(a,b]}\text{,}\) \(\tcboxmath{[a,\infty)}\text{,}\) and \(\tcboxmath{(-\infty,b]}\text{.}\) Intervals of the form \((a,b)\text{,}\) \((-\infty,b)\text{,}\) \((a,\infty)\text{,}\) and \((-\infty,\infty)\) are called open intervals while \([a,b]\) is referred to as a closed interval. A bounded interval is any interval of the form \((a,b)\text{,}\) \([a,b)\text{,}\) \((a,b]\text{,}\) and \([a,b]\text{.}\) For bounded intervals, \(a\) and \(b\) are called the endpoints of the interval.
